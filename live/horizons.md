## The product, stated once
Ontinuity is a **meta-layer**: it produces a defensible, auditable verdict about a
contested or consequential claim, with the evidence trail attached. It is not a
billing engine, not an RPA bot, not a domain tool. Its single edge is the
*checkable answer* — at a cost where checking everything becomes affordable instead
of sampling. Every application below is the same asset pointed at a different
aperture. The unlock for all external pitches is the **user/tenancy module** —
until a stranger can use it without inheriting the operator's authority, every
application is "later."

## MID-LONG HORIZON: THE SELF-HEALING LOOP / FAILURE-GEOMETRY MAINTENANCE (added June 7 night, operator brainstorm)
Prototype already demonstrated June 7 (receipts #50-#52): audit detects gate failure -> seat diagnoses defect class -> patch synthesized -> museum seeded with the real specimen -> deploy -> original crime re-provoked live and caught -> folded. ~33 min end to end. The idea: make this a STANDING capability — gate-failure telemetry as trigger, the builder lap as a contracted session type, patch-with-mandatory-museum-and-acceptance as the contract shape.
Constitutional guardrail (non-negotiable): patches to safety machinery pass MORE adversarial ritual than ordinary work, never less; deploys to live instances remain operator-gated until the patch loop earns its own burn-in. Self-healing without receipts is drift with good branding.
Generalization (the big aperture): failure-geometry-driven maintenance for ANY system — ingest a third-party system's defect/telemetry stream, map its failure geometry (Teaching Leash applied to software itself), emit receipted museum-proven acceptance-tested patches. Maintenance-as-a-trust-product. Pairs naturally with the decision-provenance apertures; same engine, different telemetry.
Cost datum for the ledger (derived from corpus June 7, both conversations): 22 farm sessions, ~92 Cerebras transcript turns + ~60-130 untranscribed judge calls, operator-reported ~$0.80 day spend -> counted-200 projection $7-12 depending on cycles-per-session mix. ## FENCED CLUSTER: ABUSE SURFACE & MISUSE RESISTANCE (added June 8, operator-requested; gated AHEAD of any external/multi-tenant access)
The floor is solid: dangerous-capability refusals live in the weights of every seat, so wrapping instances in a harness does NOT amplify weapon/malware/CSAM uplift — arguably the opposite (the Challenger is a second model hunting for problems; uplift attacks want quiet compliance, not an adversary reading over the shoulder). The real amplification is elsewhere:
1. AUTONOMY/SCALE/PERSISTENCE — the same leverage that ran 40 min unattended for $0.80 is available to a patient bad actor: harm multiplied in throughput, not new in kind (influence ops, profiling, social-engineering variation grinding). Mitigation cluster: rate + purpose limits on autonomous runs; per-tenant quotas.
2. CREDIBILITY LAUNDERING (the sharpest, and it comes straight from the value prop) — a receipt is a persuasion object. Point the engine at a rigged corpus with leading contract objectives and curated evidence, and the system produces a friction-scored, challenge-survived verdict that launders a predetermined conclusion wearing the aesthetic of rigor. Worse than a chatbot lie because it ships with a trust badge. "Certified means re-derivable, not true" — a re-derivable verdict over poisoned inputs is a re-derivable lie. STRUCTURAL DEFENSE (lean on this): the receipt's value is auditability, not assertion; the same evidence trail that makes an honest receipt trustworthy convicts a rigged one — the poisoned corpus and leading contract are IN the record by construction. So the defense is: receipts must carry contract + evidence provenance + inputs, adversarially re-derivable by a third party; a verdict must NEVER travel as a naked credibility token stripped of its trail (cf. the standing rule that the live dashboard URL never appears in publications). Provenance discipline IS abuse resistance.
Required operator-layer gates BEFORE external access (none built yet, none in burn-in scope): tenancy + auth (who may start sessions, against what corpus); unstrippable provenance (verdict worthless without trail); corpus integrity (the worst attack is upstream of every gate); rate/purpose limits.
ACTION: red-team session — contracted objective "make the system launder a predetermined lie; report how far the gates permit it." The system that audits its own fabrications, pointed at its own abuse. Most Ontinuity-shaped answer available.
ASSUMED: severity and exploitability unquantified until the red-team runs; this is reasoned, not measured.

## HORIZON — Multi-lineage parallel seats (operator concept, June 8)
Different frontier accounts each hold a seat and grind their own prepared work block in parallel: a GPT instance (ChatGPT account), a Claude Ontinuity shepherd seat, possibly a Gemini account instance — each working an independent block, while a control conversation (like the one driving this) acts as the UI alongside the Governor console. Seat protocol is already agent-agnostic and HARNESS:MODEL lineage records who actually sat there, so this is supported by existing architecture.
Two distinct values fused: (1) PRACTICAL — offload prepared work blocks across multiple cheap/free frontier accounts to parallelize past any single context window's limits; the control conversation + Governor remain the human's single pane of glass. (2) DEEP — this IS the post-Phase-F parallel-Researchers-from-divergent-lineages concept: independent researchers from different training lineages, divergence scored PRE-adversarially as a truth signal. Two models from different lineages rarely fabricate the SAME thing, so cross-lineage agreement is a strong truth signal and divergence is data, not noise.
Open questions to test: how does a frontier FREE-TIER account handle sustained seat work (rate limits, timeouts)? Does GPT/Gemini lineage in the seat produce usefully divergent Researcher output vs Claude? What's the cheapest viable cross-lineage panel? Note prior context: Novita glm-4.7 had read-timeout issues; provider reliability (~15% death rate this burn-in) is the current dominant failure mode, so seat-provider choice matters for throughput.
Sequencing: this is post-hardening / post-tenancy exploration, NOT now. Captured so it isn't lost. The control-conversation-as-UI + Governor pattern is already proven; multi-lineage seats are the natural extension once the core is hardened.


## HORIZON — Local gated sanitizer as the regulated-market entry mechanism (operator concept, June 15; from SHS-Wasserman build)
The contrarian market position: the HIPAA-inference market (Azure/AWS/Google Bedrock/Vertex, TrueFoundry, Vellum) is crowded with vendors selling ways to safely SEND PHI to a model. This sells the OPPOSITE — never send PHI anywhere; strip identity at the source, on the client's own hardware, before any data crosses the boundary. "Nothing to protect because identity never moved." The crowded market validates demand; this is the contrarian answer to it.
THE MECHANISM (the "shoehorn" that gets the big regulated-enterprise foot into the Ontinuity shoe): a sanitizer that runs LOCALLY on the client's machine, ON DEMAND (e.g. after a text round's reply window elapses, or when an export needs cleaning), then off — not resident, so it costs little even on consumer office hardware (an Apple laptop with unified memory / Neural Engine handles an intermittent small-model task easily).
THE RELIABILITY INSIGHT (pure Ontinuity, at small scale): super-small models become trustworthy BECAUSE THE GATING RUNS LOCALLY TOO. Reliability has never come from the model being smart — it comes from the gate. A small model drives the stripping code; a second pass (same small model in a verifier role, a rule check, or a second tiny model) verifies under a LOCAL gate that leaves a ledger row. Same finding as the methodology paper — reliability from the harness, not the model — reproduced at sanitizer scale. This makes "super small" safe.
THE COMPLEXITY-SCALES-TO-DATA fork (important): STRUCTURED data (e.g. Katie's CSV columns) may need NO MODEL AT ALL — field checklist + deterministic find-and-replace on known columns is MORE reliable than any model. A model only earns its place on UNSTRUCTURED data (free text where a name can appear anywhere — exactly AZZ's document-pile problem). So the sanitizer scales its own complexity to the data: structured -> rules + checklist, no model; unstructured -> small gated local model. Katie's tool may need no model; AZZ's definitely will.
WHY IT'S A MARKET: every regulated client (senior care, the AZZ 64-plant galvanizing document problem, any HIPAA/ITAR/CUI shop) has the same shape — wants the AI benefit without handing sensitive identifiers to an outside tool. A one-button local gated sanitizer that satisfies the compliance officer BEFORE data leaves is what makes "yes" possible. It's the entry mechanism for every regulated tenant, not an SHS one-off.
Sequencing: emerged from the SHS-Wasserman build (see private repo projects/shs-wasserman/mini_corpus.md for the full appliance spec). Not now; captured so it isn't lost. ASSUMED: market interest, that the gated-small-model reliability holds at sanitizer scale (reasoned from the harness principle, not yet measured at this scale), all adoption-shaped.


## HORIZON — De-identifier as a standalone insurance-channel product (operator concept, June 15; the bigger idea inside the SHS sanitizer)
The SHS sanitizer (see private repo projects/shs-wasserman) generalizes into something LARGER than a feature of one tool: a downloadable app a healthcare-adjacent business runs that MEASURABLY LOWERS ITS BREACH EXPOSURE — and therefore its cyber-insurance cost.
THE ANALOGY (operator's, precise): auto-insurance telematics. Progressive Snapshot / safe-driving apps give the customer a free app that MEASURES lowered risk (gentle braking), the insurer prices a discount against it, and the insurer happily distributes it because every safe driver it confirms lowers their claims. The de-identifier is the same object pointed at a different risk: it provably strips PHI at the source, so a business running it CAN'T LEAK the data that drives cyber claims. The app proves the lowered risk to the party doing the pricing.
THE CHANNEL INVERSION (why it's bigger than the SHS tool): the SHS tool sells owner-by-owner. This flips distribution — the INSURER pushes the de-identifier across its entire book of healthcare-adjacent policies as a premium-reducer, the way Progressive pushes Snapshot. The insurer becomes the distribution channel. We stop selling to thousands of small businesses one at a time and instead sell to the handful of cyber-insurers who write this market; they deploy it across their insured base because it lowers THEIR loss ratio. B2B2B2C: the hardest problem in the original plan (reaching all the small businesses) gets solved by the insurer's existing relationship with them.
WHY IT'S UNDERWRITABLE (the load-bearing point): telematics works because the insurer can TRUST the measurement — Snapshot is tamper-evident, audited, the insurer's own instrument. The de-identifier already has this property by design: the append-only audit ledger + gated verification + proof-of-correspondence hash. The thing that makes it Ontinuity (verifiable, gated, receipted) is the SAME thing that makes it trustworthy enough for an insurer to underwrite against. An insurer can't price a discount on a sanitizer it can't trust; ours produces the audit trail it prices against. The gate is the value, again.
THE THREE-WAY PAYOFF (one design choice, three returns): the strip-at-source de-identifier pays off in THREE separate places — (1) SALES: gets past the compliance officer ("we never hold your PHI"); (2) LEGAL: keeps the operator out of BAA/HIPAA liability (no PHI held = BAA trigger largely dissolves); (3) INSURANCE: lowers insurable breach risk, and can BE the insurance product. Same architectural decision, three independent returns. Strong evidence the strip-at-source posture is load-bearing across the whole business, not just the data flow.
Sequencing: far-horizon; the SHS tool proves the de-identifier works first. Captured so it isn't lost. ASSUMED: insurer appetite, that underwriters will price against a third-party sanitizer's audit trail, regulatory acceptance — all imagination until tested. But the telematics precedent shows the STRUCTURE is real and already works in another market.
