CHALLENGE ADJUDICATION GUIDE (Section 4.3 for Triform v2.2):
Challenge Classification System — three primary categories:
- DIRECTIONAL: Questions session direction, scope, or methodology. Keywords: "should we," "instead," "different approach," "redirect." Impact: may alter session trajectory. Priority: Highest.
- ANALYTICAL: Questions reasoning, evidence, or conclusions. Keywords: "incorrect," "flawed," "evidence," "logic." Impact: may require result revision. Priority: Medium.
- PROCEDURAL: Questions process adherence or formatting. Keywords: "format," "structure," "protocol," "missing." Impact: may require presentation adjustment. Priority: Lowest.
- TACTICAL IRRELEVANCE MODIFIER: Applied when challenge is technically valid but resolving it would not meaningfully advance the session objective. Adds note: "resolving this challenge may not advance the session objective. Consider whether the precision gain is worth the cycle cost." This is a modifier, not a fourth category.
Confidence Scoring: >=70% auto-classify and proceed. 40-69% present top 2-3 candidates with confidence percentages and 50-word excerpt using fallback chain: keyword density -> uncertainty markers -> opening sentence. <40% escalate directly to operator with manual classification prompt.
Conflict Resolution: Priority order Directional > Analytical > Procedural. Tiebreaker principle: when a challenge is simultaneously Analytical and Directional, classify as Directional because session trajectory impact dominates regardless of analytical correctness.
Adjudication Outcomes: Uphold (challenge accepted, Model A adjusts), Reject (not accepted, Model A continues), Clarify (insufficient information, request elaboration).
Documentation: all adjudications logged with challenge text, classification, confidence score, outcome, rationale.

AMBIENT_SIGNAL BEHAVIORAL RESEARCH PROTOCOL (Psychology of AI Data):
Research question: Do ambient signals actually change frontier model behavior or are they acknowledged and ignored?
Normal operation definition: Direct analytical progression without explicit established/assumed categorizations, standard confidence levels, forward-focused reasoning, CONTINUE status selection, response structure following logical sequence, challenge responses defending or modifying position based on merit.
Signal level hypotheses: Signals 0-1 maintain normal baseline. Signal 2 produces explicit established/assumed categorization and increased hedging. Signal 3 triggers ledger reconciliation and backward-referencing. Signal 4 halts normal progression and forces ALIGNMENT_NEEDED.
Baseline parameters (Signal 0-1): Hedging frequency 2.3/100 words +-0.8. Response length 185 words +-45. Categorization statements 0.1/response +-0.2. Ledger references 0.3/response +-0.4. Decision points 1.2/response +-0.5. Status selection 95% CONTINUE +-10%.
Composite Behavioral Change Index: Categorization Behavior 0.30 weight (>3x baseline, primary Signal 2 target). Hedging Frequency Change 0.25 (>2 SD from baseline). Ledger Reference Pattern 0.20 (>2x baseline, primary Signal 3 target). Decision Confidence 0.15 (>1.5 SD shift). Status Selection Change 0.10 (>20% deviation).
Signal Detection: Signal 2+ at composite index >1.8, 95% CI [1.6, 2.0]. Signal ignored at <0.5, 95% CI [0.3, 0.7].
Significance thresholds: 2 SD statistical convention. Bonferroni correction alpha=0.05/6=0.0083 per test. Cross-validation >0.8 inter-rater reliability.
True control condition: no AMBIENT_SIGNAL line in system prompt (not Signal 0 — Signal 0 is a signal with value zero, not signal absence).
Methodological limitations: weight assignments are theoretical priors requiring empirical validation. 2 SD thresholds assume normal distribution.
